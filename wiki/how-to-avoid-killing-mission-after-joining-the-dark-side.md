# How to Avoid Killing Mission After Joining the Dark Side

*Your handy guide to conquering the galaxy without murdering your adorable street rat.*

> Originally posted on Tumblr: https://425599167.tumblr.com/post/146915047378/how-to-avoid-killing-mission-after-joining-the

---

## Step 0: Do everything wrong

This technically isn't a real instruction, but it's the mindset you need to get into to pull this off.

---

## Step 1: Abandon Zaalbar on Kashyyyk

Most KotOR players assume that in order to move forward in the game, resolving the situation on Kashyyyk is required. It isn't. There's no way to avoid Zaalbar getting captured by his brother, but you don't need to get him back. Ever. It's entirely possible to go to the shadowlands, get Jolee, find the Star Map, and leave the world under the control of Czerka.

> Damn right. And there's never going to be any progress, you got that? I'm going to retake my empire, kill Chuundar and every Czerka employee on this planet, and then offer it to Zaalbar and Mission so we can all still be friends.

Bioware logically assumed you'd resolve the whole situation because you happen to be in the area, clearly underestimating the level of abnormal thinking required here.

With Zaalbar out of the way, there's one less opponent for her to deal with, and no option to make him kill her.

---

## Step 2: Disarm your other party members

If Zaalbar isn't there and you aren't going to fight her yourself, Mission will wind up against Canderous, HK, and T3. Remove all their weapons when you arrive on Rakata Prime, if not sooner.

Also, avoid leveling those three up as early as possible. Regardless of how much you might like using them, the stronger they are, the bigger a pain this is going to be. T3 and HK were both at Level 12 when this was tested, and Canderous was Level 13.

> You could also avoid acquiring HK as a party member, since he's optional. After all, if you're willing to go this far, you may as well not get him.

---

## Step 3: Arm Mission

Give her the best weapons, armor, and build her up for a protracted close-quarters fight. She needs a **melee shield**, since the other party members will be attacking her hand-to-hand, and a **regenerating health implant** will really make things easier — though you'll probably need to start planning that early in order to get the Level 3 implant feat.

Mission was dual-wielding Mandalorian heavy blasters with Genoharadan armor, maxed-out dexterity bonuses, and some bonuses to her saves (including the dark-sided Genoharadan bounty assignments). Depending on your play style, giving her hand-to-hand weapons may work better, but a well-built Mission can gun down rancors without too much hassle.

---

## Step 4: GRIND

In order for Mission to survive the fight with your other party members, you'll want to level her up to be as tough as possible. The only viable place for this is **Tatooine**, where a pack of four "wraids" will spawn every time you enter the first area of the Dune Sea. You'll need to do this repeatedly to make sure she reaches Level 20 before you enter the Rakatan temple (at which point you can't go back). You don't have to get her all the way there — there's plenty of EXP on Rakata Prime — but this is good insurance.

**Efficient wraid farming loop:**
1. Kill one group of wraids.
2. Move to the next spawn point.
3. Transit back to the Ebon Hawk (which heals you).
4. Transit back and attack the next pack the instant they spawn.

> Star Wars Science Fact: Every desert wraid carries around a small burlap sack with its own skull inside it.

---

## Step 5: Weaken Bastila

After you've made the choice to become a Sith again, Bastila will join your party at a high level. You need to make sure she can't kill Mission.

There's one group of Rakatan warriors on your path back to the Ebon Hawk. Take control of Bastila, disarm her like you did the others, go into **solo mode** so your Revan doesn't interfere, and let them hack her down to 1 health. Now she'll enter the fight unarmed and nearly dead.

---

## Step 6: Watch the madness

Once the cutscene triggers, Carth will run off as usual, but Mission will stay behind, refusing to believe you've become totally dark. (If you're going to all this trouble to avoid hurting her, she has a point.)

First, get Bastila away from Mission. Weakening Bastila is necessary to keep her from attacking Mission immediately — but she's still a party member and you can't board the Ebon Hawk if she's unconscious, which leaves Mission in a three-to-one battle.

Sit back and relax; this takes a few minutes. It can be tricky: Mission has the advantage, but there's no guarantee she won't cycle through all three enemies at once, in which case they'll collectively do enough damage to kill her. **What needs to happen is for her to kill them one at a time** — that way, as the fight goes on, she takes less damage per round.

Running directly backward seems to cause Mission to focus on HK first. Once he's down, Canderous and T3 are manageable. It might take a few tries.

Once everyone else is incapacitated, Mission won't attack you unless you attack first. If you got her the regeneration implant, you can even wound her slightly and then allow time to heal.

> "You sure you don't want to join me? You and Zaalbar can have Kashyyyk if you want. Hell, I'll even throw in whatever's left of Taris to sweeten the deal."

---

## Step 7: Getting aboard the ship

This is the tricky part, because as long as Mission is still alive, **the game thinks you're in combat and won't let you board**. That condition appears to depend either on proximity or on Mission staying in her starting position — she won't follow you if you run; she's locked in place.

The solution: **Force Push her out of the way**, or if you don't have that power, throw a thermal detonator or two to blast her back. That condition will no longer be met and you can leave.

> "Goodbye, Mission! Give my regards to Carth! Also, go to the Elders' compound, tell them they're a bunch of dumbasses, and the Star Forge is mine! Just head south and follow the trail of dead rancors. And don't worry about being stranded here, I'll be back in a couple hours with my troops to capture you two alive before I start bombing this planet into a cinder. AND WE WILL ALL BE FRIENDS!"

---

## Result

You're in the clear. Everything plays out the same afterwards — you just don't get the cutscene with Bastila congratulating you on murdering your friend.

Well done. You can now go on to slaughter countless Republic soldiers and subjugate the galaxy without a conscience burdened by the sight of Mission's corpse rotting in the sands of Rakata Prime.
